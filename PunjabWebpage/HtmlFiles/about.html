About Punjab

The Land of Five Rivers

Introduction

Punjab is a culturally rich and agriculturally prosperous state in northern India. Its name originates from the Persian words "Panj" meaning five, and "Aab" meaning water — referring to the five rivers flowing through the region.

Geography & Capital

Covering around 50,362 square kilometers, Punjab borders Pakistan and Indian states like Haryana and Himachal Pradesh. Its capital, Chandigarh, is a well-planned union territory shared with Haryana.

People & Culture

The people of Punjab are known for their vibrant clothing, heartwarming hospitality, and lively celebrations. Folk dances like Bhangra and Giddha reflect the state’s energy and joy.

Religion & Spirituality

Punjab is the birthplace of Sikhism. The Golden Temple in Amritsar is the holiest shrine for Sikhs and a symbol of peace and devotion. People also celebrate diverse religious festivals.

Agriculture & Economy

Often called the Granary of India, Punjab contributes heavily to India’s wheat and rice production. Its fertile lands, hardworking farmers, and efficient irrigation make it agriculturally strong.
