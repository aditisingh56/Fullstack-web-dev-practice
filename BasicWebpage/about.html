About Punjab

The Land of Five Rivers

Introduction

Punjab is a culturally rich and agriculturally prosperous state in northern India. Its name originates from the Persian words "Panj" meaning five, and "Aab" meaning water, referring to the five rivers — Satluj, Beas, Ravi, Chenab, and Jhelum — that flow through the region.

Geography & Capital

The state covers an area of approximately 50,362 square kilometers and shares its border with Pakistan. The capital of Punjab is Chandigarh, which is also a union territory and the capital of Haryana.

People & Culture

Punjabis are known for their warm hospitality, vibrant festivals, and energetic music and dance forms like Bhangra and Giddha. The major spoken language is Punjabi, written in the Gurmukhi script.

Religion & Spirituality

Punjab is the birthplace of Sikhism, and is home to the holiest shrine in Sikhism — the Golden Temple in Amritsar. The state also celebrates diverse religious and cultural events with great enthusiasm.

Agriculture & Economy

Known as the "Granary of India", Punjab plays a vital role in the country’s food grain production. Its fertile soil and extensive irrigation systems have made it one of India’s leading agricultural states.
